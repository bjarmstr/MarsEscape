Safety Refresher

Oxygen/Carbon Dioxide/Sabatier Cycle

Oxygen Generating Assembly

Combine water (H2O) and electricity to produce oxygen (O2) and hydrogen (H2) (2H2O + electricity → 2H2 + O2).

Hydrogen is very flammable and storing it a challenge. The Sabatier Reactor immediately consumes the H2 gas so safe storage is not required.

Sabatier Reactor Assembly

Combine Hydrogen (H2) and Carbon Dioxide (CO2) together to produce water (H2O) and Methane (CH4) (4H2 + CO2 → 2H2O + CH4). Water is not easily obtained on Mars so water recovery by the SRA is an essential part of the cycle.

The H2 gas is very flammable so piping and components in the SRA must have all traces of the H2 gas removed before the reactor is started. A nitrogen purge is used for this purpose.

Carbon Dioxide Removal Assembly

The Mars atmosphere contains 95% CO2 compared to .04% on Earth. Low level exposure to CO2 can cause headaches, dizziness, and can make it hard to concentrate. At higher levels, CO2 gas is fatal.

Remove Carbon Dioxide by blowing air through a bed of zeolite rocks. CO2 and water stick to the zeolite, while everything else passes through.

Water Processing Assembly

High quality water, safe from contaminants is required for the Oxygen Generating Assembly. Colonists also require drinking water to survive. The WPA produces clean potable water using physical and chemical processes, including filtration and temperature sterilization.

Environmental Controls

The Environmental Controls monitors and controls the life support systems to ensure a habitable environment. Air pressure, CO2 and O2 percentages, temperature and humidity are all being constantly monitored.
